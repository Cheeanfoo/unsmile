What is Oral Spray

An oral spray is a liquid solution that is delivered into the mouth through a spray nozzle. It is used to administer medications, vitamins, or supplements by spraying directly onto the tissues inside the mouth, where it is absorbed quickly through the mucous membranes.

Information

“Oral sprays are a convenient way to deliver medication, vitamins, or supplements by spraying them directly into the mouth. They offer faster absorption through the mouth’s mucous membranes, bypassing the digestive system for quicker effects. Popular types include pain relief sprays, vitamin sprays, and breath fresheners. They are especially useful for people who have difficulty swallowing pills and provide precise dosing. While easy to use, potential side effects include local irritation or allergic reactions, and it’s important to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for medicated sprays.”

Leong

CEO of Company

“Oral sprays are often preferred for their portability and ease of use, making them ideal for quick, on-the-go administration. Unlike pills or capsules, oral sprays don’t require water, and they can be absorbed faster into the bloodstream through the tissues in the mouth. This makes them particularly effective for situations where rapid relief is needed, such as pain management or providing a vitamin boost.”

Foo

Secretary Of Company

“Common ingredients in oral sprays include vitamins like B12 or D3, herbal extracts, and medications like nitroglycerin or lidocaine for pain relief. They provide precise dosing, ensuring users get the exact amount needed with each spray. However, some users may experience irritation or an unpleasant taste, and it's important to check for allergies and consult with a healthcare professional before using any new oral spray product.”

Chloe

Assitant Of Company
